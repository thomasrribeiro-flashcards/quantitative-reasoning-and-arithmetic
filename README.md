# Quantitative Reasoning and Arithmetic

Novice-first spaced-repetition deck for reasoning with everyday quantities,
representations, arithmetic, estimates, and checks.

## Scope

- Subject: `mathematics`
- Learner level: `foundational`
- Confirmed mathematical/tool prerequisites: none
- Confirmed subject prerequisites: none
- Machine-readable deck and tool prerequisites: `deck.toml`
- What mastery enables: interpret and compare quantities; compute and estimate
  with whole, signed, fractional, decimal, percent, and proportional quantities;
  translate among words, notation, number lines, tables, and short quantitative
  situations; select a method; diagnose common errors; and check whether an
  answer is reasonable.
- Deliberate exclusions: formal algebra and function notation, geometric
  formulas beyond arithmetic measurement contexts, statistics and probability,
  calculator-specific procedures, and extended multi-step projects. Those
  belong in later decks or practice outside SRS.

Estimation, representation translation, and error diagnosis recur throughout
rather than appearing only at the end. SRS maintains short decisions and
procedures; it does not replace sustained written and mental arithmetic
practice.

## Chapter map

Chapter 1 is the approved pilot. Human approval authorizes the complete
ten-chapter curriculum below; later chapters preserve the pilot and extend its
explicit prerequisite chain.

| File | Topic | Learning outcomes |
|---|---|---|
| `flashcards/01_quantities_and_whole_numbers.md` | Quantities and whole-number sense | Count reliably; distinguish quantity, numeral, digit, and whole number; interpret place value and zero; compare on a number line; distinguish exact values from estimates; round to nearby tens and hundreds. |
| `flashcards/02_addition_and_subtraction.md` | Joining, separating, and comparing | Model addition and subtraction; choose the operation from context; compute whole-number sums and differences; use inverse operations, estimation, and bounds to diagnose errors. |
| `flashcards/03_multiplication_and_division.md` | Equal groups and sharing | Translate among equal groups, arrays, multiplication, sharing, and grouping division; compute products and quotients; interpret remainders; check with inverse operations and estimates. |
| `flashcards/04_operation_structure_and_factors.md` | Expressions, factors, and powers | Use operation order and grouping marks; recognize multiples and factors; apply divisibility reasoning; interpret primes, composites, and small whole-number powers without introducing algebra. |
| `flashcards/05_signed_quantities.md` | Positive, negative, and zero | Interpret signed quantities as position and change; compare them on a number line; add and subtract using direction and distance; diagnose sign errors in authentic contexts. |
| `flashcards/06_fractions.md` | Fractions as quantities and operators | Interpret fractions as equal parts, points, quotients, and “of” operators; generate equivalents; compare; compute with fractions; estimate and test reasonableness. |
| `flashcards/07_decimals.md` | Decimal place value and computation | Translate between finite decimals, fractions, money, and measurement; compare and round decimals; compute with place-value alignment; diagnose calculator and alignment errors. |
| `flashcards/08_ratios_rates_and_proportions.md` | Multiplicative comparison | Distinguish additive from multiplicative comparison; interpret ratios and unit rates; use tables and scaling; solve proportional problems; reject nonproportional shortcuts. |
| `flashcards/09_percents.md` | Percent as “per hundred” | Translate among percent, fraction, and decimal forms; find parts, wholes, and rates; reason about discounts, tax, tips, and change; distinguish percentage points from percent change. |
| `flashcards/10_measurement_estimation_and_decisions.md` | Units, precision, and mixed quantitative reasoning | Choose and convert compatible units; distinguish exact counts from measurements; report sensible precision; propagate simple rounding effects; solve and critique mixed authentic problems. |

Chapter prerequisite edges and provided concepts live only in chapter
frontmatter. Inspect the generated human-readable graph with
`flashcards deck prerequisites .`; do not maintain a second prose graph here.
